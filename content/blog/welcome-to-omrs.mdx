When I started my residency ten years ago, I spent more time wrestling with our hospital's Electronic Health Record (EHR) system than I did looking my patients in the eye. 

The software was clunky, slow, and clearly designed for billing rather than clinical care. Important clinical data was buried under layers of sub-menus, leading to missed insights and near-miss errors.

### The Problem with Legacy Systems

Most healthcare software today suffers from three fatal flaws:
1. **Built for Billing, Not Care:** They are glorified cash registers.
2. **Closed Ecosystems:** They refuse to talk to other systems.
3. **Terrible UX:** They require 20 clicks to do something that should take 2.

<Callout type="warning">
  Up to 40% of a physician's day is spent navigating poor user interfaces, directly leading to physician burnout.
</Callout>

### Enter OMRS

We decided to build something different. **OMRS** (Online Medical Record System) is designed from the ground up to be:
- **Fast:** Sub-200ms searches.
- **Intuitive:** A modern interface that feels like the consumer apps you use every day.
- **Secure:** End-to-end encryption and cryptographic audit logs.

We're just getting started. Welcome to the future of healthcare.
